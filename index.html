Why visiting Granada?

Granada, home city of La Alhambra

The Alhambra above is Granada's crown jewel — a sprawling Moorish palace and fortress overlooking the city, famous for its intricate tilework, reflecting pools, and views over the Albaicín's white-washed streets to the snow-capped Sierra Nevada beyond. Granada is worth visiting for the rare layering of cultures you can feel just by walking through it: centuries of Islamic, Christian, and Roma influence sit side by side, from the Alhambra and the Generalife gardens to the labyrinthine Albaicín quarter and the flamenco caves of Sacromonte. Add in a lively tapas culture (still free with every drink in many bars), affordable prices compared to Spain's bigger cities, and a backdrop of mountains close enough for a day trip to ski or hike, and you get a city that rewards slow wandering as much as sightseeing.
